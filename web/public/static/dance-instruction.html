Thoughts on Dance Instruction

Teaching dance is both an art and a science. Over years of instruction, I've developed perspectives on what makes dance education effective and enjoyable.

Philosophy of Teaching

"The best teachers don't just teach steps—they teach how to learn."

My approach to dance instruction emphasizes:

  • Building foundations — Strong fundamentals enable creativity
  • Musical awareness — Dance is a conversation with the music
  • Connection and lead/follow — Partner dancing is about communication
  • Individual expression — Everyone brings their own style

Learning Principles

Key principles that apply to learning any dance style:

  • Practice deliberately — Quality over quantity in repetition
  • Break down complex movements — Master the parts, then combine
  • Video yourself — Objective feedback accelerates improvement
  • Dance socially — Real-world practice is irreplaceable
  • Learn from multiple instructors — Different perspectives enrich understanding

Common Challenges

Challenges I frequently help students work through:

  • Overthinking instead of feeling the music
  • Tension in the frame or body
  • Rushing through movements
  • Comparing progress to others
